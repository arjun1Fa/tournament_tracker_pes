# EFL OFFICIAL RULEBOOK – SEASON I

## eFootball Competitive League (EFL)

A ten-club round-robin competition with a structured knockout stage. This document governs all proceedings of the league from the group phase through to the Grand Final.

---

# ARTICLE 01 – League Overview

## ART. 01.1 – Identity

The competition shall be officially known as the **eFootball League (EFL)**. It is a private, organised eFootball (PES/eFootball) tournament contested between ten (10) participants, each managing a licensed club side on the eFootball platform.

## ART. 01.2 – Structure at a Glance

The EFL is played across two sequential stages:

**Stage I — League Phase**

* Full home-and-away round-robin among all ten clubs.

**Stage II — Knockout Phase**

* Six-team elimination bracket seeded directly from League Phase standings.
* Culminates in the Grand Final.

## ART. 01.3 – Platform & Settings

All matches must be played on eFootball. Match settings (half length, difficulty, conditions) must remain consistent across the competition and are to be agreed upon before the season commences.

Any deviation requires mutual consent and must be reported to the league administrator.

# ARTICLE 03 – The League Phase

## ART. 03.1 – Format

The League Phase follows a double round-robin format, identical in structure to the English Premier League.

Every club plays every other club twice:

* Once as the designated home side
* Once as the designated away side

With ten clubs, each club shall complete exactly:

* 18 matches total
* 9 home matches
* 9 away matches

## ART. 03.2 – Points System

| Result | Points | Description                       |
| ------ | ------ | --------------------------------- |
| Win    | 3      | Match won by any margin           |
| Draw   | 1      | Match ends level after 90 minutes |
| Loss   | 0      | Match lost by any margin          |

## ART. 03.3 – League Table

Standings are maintained throughout the League Phase and updated after every match.

The table shall display:

* Matches Played (MP)
* Wins (W)
* Draws (D)
* Losses (L)
* Goals For (GF)
* Goals Against (GA)
* Goal Difference (GD)
* Points (Pts)

## ART. 03.4 – Scheduling & Walkovers

Matches must be played within a mutually agreed deadline.

If a participant fails to arrange their scheduled match within the allotted window and does not provide adequate notice, the league administrator reserves the right to award a **3–0 walkover (forfeit)** in favour of the available opponent.

# ARTICLE 04 – Tiebreaker Rules

## ART. 04.1 – Determining League Positions

When two or more clubs are level on points, the following criteria are applied in strict sequential order:

### 1. Goal Difference (GD)

The club with the superior goal difference (Goals For minus Goals Against across all League Phase matches) is ranked higher.

If still equal, proceed to criterion 2.

### 2. Goals Scored (GF)

The club that has scored the greater total number of goals across all League Phase matches is ranked higher.

If still equal, proceed to criterion 3.

### 3. Head-to-Head Record

The results of the direct matches played between the tied clubs are considered.

The club with more points in those head-to-head fixtures is ranked higher.

If multiple clubs are tied:

1. Head-to-head goal difference
2. Head-to-head goals scored

If the tie persists, it is referred to the league administrator for resolution.

### Note

Tiebreaker criteria apply exclusively to determining league table positions.

In Knockout Phase matches (including qualifiers, semi-finals, and the final), there is no draw.

# ARTICLE 05 – The Knockout Phase

## ART. 05.1 – Qualification

Upon conclusion of the League Phase, the top six (6) clubs in the final standings qualify for the Knockout Phase.

Clubs finishing 7th through 10th are eliminated from the competition.

## ART. 05.2 – Knockout Bracket Structure

The Knockout Phase consists of three rounds:

**Qualification Round → Semi-Finals → Grand Final**

### Qualification Round

* 3rd Place vs 5th Place
* 4th Place vs 6th Place

### Semi-Finals

* 1st Place vs Winner of (4th vs 6th)
* 2nd Place vs Winner of (3rd vs 5th)

### Grand Final

* Semi-Final 1 Winner vs Semi-Final 2 Winner

### Seeding Logic

The Q1 winner (3rd vs 5th) faces the 2nd-place club in the semi-finals.

The Q2 winner (4th vs 6th) faces the 1st-place club.

This rewards the league leader with the theoretically easier path to the final.

## ART. 05.3 – Knockout Match Resolution

All Knockout Phase matches are single-leg fixtures.

There are no draws in the Knockout Phase.

If a match is level after 90 minutes:

1. Extra Time
2. Penalty Shootout (if still level)

Standard eFootball mechanics apply.

## ART. 05.4 – The Grand Final

The Grand Final is contested between the two Semi-Final winners.

The winner is crowned:

**EFL Season I Champion**

The title is held until dethroned in a subsequent season.

The losing finalist receives the **Runner-Up** distinction.

# ARTICLE 06 – Conduct & Fair Play

## ART. 06.1 – Sportsmanship

All participants are expected to maintain respectful conduct throughout the competition.

Unsportsmanlike behaviour, including:

* Intentional disconnections
* Deliberate exploitation of known game bugs
* Harassment of opponents

may result in:

* Match forfeit
* Points deduction
* Expulsion

at the discretion of the league administrator.

## ART. 06.2 – Disconnections & Restarts

In the event of an unintentional disconnection, both parties must attempt to restart the match from the point of disruption if technically possible.

If a restart is not achievable, the matter is referred to the league administrator, who shall make a binding ruling based on available evidence such as:

* Screenshots
* Score at time of disconnect

## ART. 06.3 – Result Reporting

Both participants are jointly responsible for reporting the final scoreline to the league administrator promptly after each match concludes.

A screenshot of the final result screen is mandatory as proof.

Unverified results may be voided.

## ART. 06.4 – Administrator Authority

The league administrator holds final authority on all matters not explicitly covered by this rulebook, including:

* Disputes
* Edge cases
* Disciplinary action

All administrator decisions are final and binding.
